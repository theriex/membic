Membic.com is the premiere platform for managing and presenting helpful links as part of your website or independently. Free for individuals and collaborative groups.
  • What's a Membic?   slides | text
    A membic has four parts:
    1. A link, which can either be a URL, or identifying field values (e.g. title and author for a book)
    2. A star rating, which reflects the quality, relevance and importance of the link to you
    3. Any keywords you want associated
    4. The reason why you thought the link was memorable
  • Your Profile   slides | text
    Your profile has a picture or icon so people can see which membics are from you. You can use your real name or a pen name. There is an optional description where you can put public information about yourself.

    Your profile has display tabs for:

    • Membics you've made recently
    • Your top membics of all time
    • Membics from others you've remembered
    • Fast search of your membics
    • Profiles you endorse
    • Themes you contribute to
  • Themes   slides | text
    Themes collect related membics. People collaborate on themes based on their membership level:
    1. Founders control all membership and content
    2. Moderators help maintain appropriate posting
    3. Members post membics to the theme
    4. Followers are interested readers

    A theme page has a picture, name, and description like a profile, along with recent, top, and enhanced search display tabs. Themes also

    • Have a permalink for direct access
    • Can be given a unique hashtag
    • Support theme-specific keywords
    • Provide visual traffic analysis
    • Have an RSS newsfeed
    • Can be embedded in another website

    Themes are a powerful and flexible way to provide helpful links from a standalone site or embedded in your own website.

  • Discover   slides | text
    The membic community display provides a way to discover themes and people. You can adjust who you want to hear from by changing the prioritization on their profile:
    1. Prefer shows you trust this person's membics and want anything they post to be displayed before everything else.
    2. Endorse shows you trust this person's membics but want to keep the standard sort order.
    3. Normal is just the system standard display filtering and sort order.
    4. Background indicates membics from this person should be displayed after membics from everyone else.
    5. Block indicates you never want to see membics from this person ever.

    You can also react to individual membics by clicking the stacked menu next to the title. That brings action buttons to

    • "Star" a membic to let the writer know it was particularly helpful, interesting, or important.
    • "Remember" a membic for later reference.
    • Write your own membic for the same link.

    Membic does not support comments or conversation, but you can post your membics to popular social media sites as you write them.

    If you are not sure what to start with, consider recommending a couple of books you think everyone should read. After that, you might mention some fun things around town, any great food or drink possibilities, videos, music, or movies. At least once every couple of weeks, consider what was the most informative article you've read and make a membic for it.

    When you find something worth remembering, make a membic.

Features:

  • Keep your search engine
    On membic.com you choose people and themes you trust, then browse their curated links and why they are memorable.
  • Keep your social net
    You don't need another social net, you need a separate collaboration space. Why?
  • Pen names
    Your pen name can reveal as much or as little of yourself as you want. Creative pen names are welcome. Your email address is never shared with anyone else.
  • Cooperative themes
    Founders, moderators and members can post membics to themes dedicated to specific interests. Themes support RSS and can be embedded into other websites.
  • Curated community
    Not only are you seeing only the links other people found memorable and why, you can prefer, background, or block people to optimize your community view. Follow specific themes you are interested in for easy access from your profile.
  • Open keyword tagging
    Tag membics using the standard keyword checkboxes, write in your own, or both. Add custom theme-specific keywords.
  • Tracking and memory
    Automatically track your recent favorite membics for easy access. Remember membics from others, or mark your own membics as future to follow up on later.

FAQ:

  • Are you going to spam me?
    No. Your email address is confirmed with the server to help verify you are a human (or at least a modestly well written robot). We send you mail if you request your password, or if someone who already knows your email address asks us to send you an invitation to collaborate on a theme they are writing. We don't share your email with anyone ever.
  • What should I start with?
    Try making membics for the last five things you encountered that were worth remembering. Books are always impressive.

Tips and Tricks:

  • Bookmarklet

    Firefox: "Right click" the 'Make Membic' link below and add it to your bookmarks.
    Chrome/Safari: "Right click" the 'Make Membic' link below, copy the link address, make a new bookmark, then paste the URL.

    Make Membic

    The next time you are on a memorable page, select "Make Membic" from your bookmarks and the script will launch membic in a new browser window.

  • Mail In Membics

    You can make a membic by sending mail to: membic@membicsys.appspotmail.com

    Describe why memorable in the subject line. In the body, the first line starting with "http://" or "https://" is used for the URL. Mailing in will create a future membic you can fill out next time you are online. Find mailed-in membics on the remembered tab of your profile.

  • iTunes® playlists

    If you would like to build playlists from keywords and update your top music membics automatically from iTunes® check out the situational playlist generator project on github.
