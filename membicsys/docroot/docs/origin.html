Not every day, but occasionally, I find something online or offline that is worth remembering. Sometimes these things come up in conversation, and I want to be able to quickly find a reference to pass along. I'm also interested in what other people find noteworthy.

Traditional social interactions are pleasant, but unfocused and low bandwidth. I'm interested in what a lot of people find memorable, but there isn't enough time in the day to exchange finds through traditional conversation. That's where web collaboration can help. A collaborative memory project.

In building this site, it was important that noting something memorable should be easy and direct. A quick note to yourself, rather than an expository critique for others. Turns out that's more interesting to read anyway. With basic identifying information, and optional tagging, the result is a bite sized structured summary of something worth remembering. A membic.

Membic.com makes every effort to be a low noise high quality collaboration space. Many discoveries are conversational, comparatively few are worth remembering. When you find something worth remembering, make a membic.
