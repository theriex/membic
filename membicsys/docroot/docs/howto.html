Community membics

When you first open membic.com, or click the main logo, you see the most recent membics posted by the community. What you see is filtered automatically for you, but if you particularly like or dislike what someone is posting you can click the under their picture to prefer, background, or block membics from them (they won't know).

If you only want to see membics of a particular type, you can use the type toggle buttons at the top of the page.

If you see a membic that looks interesting, click the title to expand actions and posting details. If the membic was helpful or remarkable (in a good way) you can it to show your appreciation. Think of "star" as kind of a hybrid "like" or "upvote" thing. You can also click to remember anything you might want to follow up on later. Making a membic

Every membic has a type to help determine what fields and keywords are most appropriate. After selecting the most appropriate type, fill in the URL if there is a page with details. Click the "Read" button to have membic.com attempt to read the URL and fill out details automatically for you (some sites work better than others).

After identifying information is filled in, use the star rating slider, the comment text area, and the keyword checkboxes to note your impressions. Think about this as writing a quick note to your future self about what you think is most memorable. If there are additional keywords you want, just type them into the keywords box separated by commas.

If you haven't finished the membic, but want to save the identifying information for later, check the "future" checkbox and you can find the membic again later by clicking (your memory).

Your membics

To access your membics, click "My membics". Membic.com automatically keeps track of your most recent membics , and your top membics of all time . The search tab allows you to quickly locate membics you've written, even if it was a long time ago. The preferred tab provides quick access to pen names you think are notable, and the themes tab holds themes you are following or are a member of.

To edit your profile details, click the gears icon next to your pen name. If you have a website or a blog you would like people to visit, you can provide a link in your profile description.

If you want to share your profile with others, click your name for convenient share buttons and your profile permalink.

Themes

If some of the membics you note are related to an ongoing interest, you can create a theme to collect them. Themes are similar to profiles in that they track recent and top membics, and can be searched, but themes can also support cooperative postings from multiple members.

To find cooperative themes, look for a "Posted to" line in the expanded membic display. If it's there, you can click the link to go to the theme, then click the follow button if you are interested in the content. If you are following, and would also like to contribute content, you can apply for membership.

Themes provide workflow for content moderation, and member management. They also support RSS, can be linked to external event calendars, and can be embedded into other websites. Click the settings button on the theme for details.

After creating or joining a theme, you'll have the option to post through to it anytime you create or edit a membic.
