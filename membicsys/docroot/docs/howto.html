Community membics

When you first open membic.com, or click the main logo, you see the most recent membics posted by the community. What you see is filtered automatically for you, but if you particularly like or dislike what someone is posting you can click the dot underneath their profile pic to prefer, background, or block membics from them (they won't know). To focus on a particular membic type, click one of the type buttons at the top of the page.

If you see a membic that looks interesting, click the title to expand actions and posting details. If the membic was helpful or remarkable (in a good way) you can it to show your appreciation. Think of "star" as kind of a hybrid "like" or "upvote" thing. You can also click to remember the membic for later if you want to follow up on it. Making a membic

Every membic has a type to help determine what fields and keywords are most appropriate. After selecting the most appropriate type, fill in the URL if there is a page with details. Click the "Read" button to have membic.com attempt to read the URL and fill out details automatically for you (some sites work better than others).

After identifying information is filled in, use the star rating slider, the comment text area, and the keyword checkboxes to note your impressions. Think about this as writing a quick note to your future self about what you think is most memorable. If there are additional keywords you want, just type them into the keywords box separated by commas.

If haven't finished the membic, but want to save the identifying information for later, check the "future" checkbox and you can find the membic again later by clicking (your memory).

Your membics

To access your membics, click "my membics". Membic.com automatically keeps track of your most recent membics () and your top membics of all time (). The search ( ) tab allows you to quickly locate membics you've written, even if it was a long time ago. The themes () tab holds themes you are following or are a member of.

To edit your profile details, click the gears icon next to your pen name. If you have a website or a blog you would like people to visit, you can give the URL in your profile description.

Themes

If some percentage of the membics you make are related to an ongoing interest of yours, you can create a theme to collect them. Themes are similar to profiles in that they track recent and top membics, and can be searched, but themes can also support cooperative postings from multiple members.

To find cooperative themes, look for a "Posted to" line in the expanded membic display. If it's there, you can click the link to go to the theme, then click the follow button if you are interested in the content. If you are following, and would also like to contribute content, you can apply for membership.

Themes provide workflow for content moderation and member management, support RSS, can be linked to external event calendars, and can be embedded into other websites.

After creating or joining a theme, you'll have the option to post through to it anytime you create or edit a membic.
