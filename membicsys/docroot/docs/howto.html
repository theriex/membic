about membic.com

Community membics

When you first open membic.com, or click the main logo, you see the most recent membics posted by the entire membic community. What you see is filtered automatically, but if you particularly like or dislike what someone is posting you can click the () under their picture to tune how visible they are to you. Prefer () sorts their posts ahead of all general posts. Endorse () shows you like what they post but doesn't change the general sort order. Background () sorts their posts below all general posts. Block () means you never see anything from them again.

If you only want to see membics of a particular type, you can use the toggle filter buttons at the top of the page.

If you see a membic that looks interesting, click the title to see more. If the membic was helpful or remarkable (in a good way) you can it to show your appreciation. Think of "star" as kind of a "like" or "upvote" thing. You can also click to remember anything you might want to follow up on later. To make your own membic from the one you are viewing, click .

Making a membic

Every membic has a type to help determine what fields and keywords are most appropriate. After selecting the most appropriate type, fill in the URL (if available) and click the "Read" button to fill out details automatically (some sites work better than others). You can also fill out details yourself.

After identifying information is filled in, write a sentence or two describing why the link is memorable. Use the star rating, and keywords to provide more organizational detail. Standard checkbox keywords for each type are provided for convenience, you can also type in any other keywords you want.

If you haven't finished the membic, but want to save the identifying information for later, check the "future" checkbox and it will be saved under your profile "remembered" tab. After saving, you can choose to share the membic to a variety of social networks.

Your profile

Your profile automatically keeps track of your most recent membics (), your top membics of all time (), and any remembered or future membics (). The search tab () allows you to quickly locate membics you've written, even if it was a long time ago. The endorsed profiles tab () provides quick access to pen names you think are worth listening to, and the themes tab () holds themes you are following or are a member of.

To edit your profile details, click the gears icon next to your pen name. If you have a website or a blog you would like people to visit, you can provide a link in your profile description.

If you want to share your profile with others, click your name for convenient share buttons and your profile permalink.

Themes

If several of your membics relate to a particular topic, you can create a theme for easy access. A theme is like a profile on steroids that can optionally be run by a whole group of people. Themes are their own web pages that can be embedded into other sites, provide a newsfeed, have their own access statistics and more.

To find themes, look for the "Posted to" line after the description of any interesting membic you find. If the membic was posted to a theme, you can click the link see it. When viewing a theme, click "Follow" to add it to your profile. After following, if you decide you want to contribute, you can apply for membership status from the settings ().

When you make a membic, you can post it through to any themes you are a member of.
