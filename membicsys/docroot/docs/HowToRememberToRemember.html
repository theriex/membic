How To

Augmented Memory

Amassing a trove of helpful links can make you feel like your positive memory has doubled, but it only works if you remember to create a membic when you discover something noteworthy. Here's how to remember to remember:

  1. When you find something memorable, don't lose it. Put it on your "to do" list. Email it to yourself. Save it to your reading. Put it on your calendar. Put it on your shopping list. Do whatever you usually do to keep track of something small that you want to deal with.
  2. Make a membic. Paste the URL and click the "Read" button, or choose a type and use the autocomplete to help fill out the details.

Extras

Most of the time you're probably reading on your phone, so you just copy the link location, open membic and paste the URL. You can add Membic to your home screen to make that easier. Here's some other fun extras to check out if they match how you internet:

  • The Membic Bookmarklet is a tiny snippet of code that takes the URL of any page you are viewing and passes it along to Membic in a new browser tab. After installing, you can select "Make Membic" from your bookmarks to start writing a membic for any page you are on. Here's how to install the bookmarklet:
    • Firefox
      1. "Right click" this link Make Membic and choose "Bookmark This Link".
    • Chrome/Safari
      1. "Right click" this link Make Membic and choose "Copy Link Address".
      2. Bookmarks | Bookmark This Page...
      3. Edit... change the bookmark name to "Make Membic", replace the URL with the copied link. Save.
    The next time you are reading something memorable, select "Make Membic" from your bookmarks to make a membic for it.

  • Mail In your membic by sending mail to membic@membicsys.appspotmail.com. Describe why it is memorable in the subject line. In the body of the mail message, the first line starting with "http://" or "https://" is used for the URL. Mailing in will create a future membic you can fill out next time you are online. Find your mailed-in membics on the remembered tab of your profile .

  • If you would like to build playlists from keywords, and update your top music membics automatically from iTunes®, check out the situational playlist generator project on github.
