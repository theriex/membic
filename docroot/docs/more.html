Weekly activity summary email

The activity summary email is now organized by review type, and you can manage the frequency from your profile account settings. By default the review summary is sent weekly, which is an easy way to keep up with reviews from friends.

If you didn't provide an email address when you first signed up, you can still add one by opening your profile settings and clicking the account settings link. Providing an email address is a good idea since you will need it if you ever need to recover your account.

Connect to wdydfun directly from iTunes on your Mac!

Now available for download are 3 scripts you can run directly from iTunes:

  • MORSetup connects iTunes to your pen name and sets up playlists for each music keyword.
  • MORKeywords provides easy keyword, comment and rating entry for the current track.
  • MORUpdate uploads your review data and refreshes the content for any keyword playlist.

Now you can review your music, sync to wdydfun, and get automatic playlists based on your own reviews, all right from iTunes! Cool!

Ok, so this is not exactly a one click install, but you'll be up and running in a minute or so...

  1. Download iTunesMac.zip
  2. Double click the downloaded iTunesMac.zip to expand it.
  3. Double click Install.scpt and it will open in the AppleScript Editor. You will see code. Don't panic. Just click the big green "Run" button and quit the AppleScript Editor after it finishes.
  4. Launch iTunes and select MORSetup from the script menu (the scrolly looking graphic thingy in the top menu bar).

That's it. You are up and running. To make it easier to bring up MORKeywords, you might want to set up a keyboard shortcut. I went into my System Preferences, opened "Keyboard" and selected "Application Shortcuts". Then I clicked the '+' on the right to create a new shortcut, selected iTunes as the application, MORKeywords as the menu title, and command-; as my keyboard shortcut (iTunes already has a lot of keybindings already). That way while I'm in iTunes I just hit command-; to bring up the MORKeywords script. Smooth.
